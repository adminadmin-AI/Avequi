# Finance Squad Output Quality Checklist

**Checklist ID:** FINANCE-CL-001
**Referenced by:** tasks/review.md
**Purpose:** Validate corporate finance deliverables for quality before delivery.

[[LLM: INITIALIZATION INSTRUCTIONS

This checklist validates finance deliverables against professional-grade standards.

EXECUTION APPROACH:
1. For each category, verify every item against the deliverable
2. Mark items as [x] Pass, [ ] Fail, [N/A] Not Applicable
3. CRITICAL items block delivery; non-critical items are advisory]]

---

## 1. Numbers & Math

- [ ] All calculations are internally consistent (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Revenue, cost, and margin relationships are correct
- [ ] Balance sheet balances (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) if applicable (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Cash flow reconciles to balance sheet changes if applicable (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Unit economics are correctly calculated (CAC, LTV, payback period)
- [ ] Percentages and multiples are applied correctly

## 2. Assumptions

- [ ] All key assumptions are made explicit, not buried in formulas (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Each assumption has a label, unit of measure, and source
- [ ] Assumptions are internally consistent (e.g., growth rates align with market size)
- [ ] Historical data is used to anchor forward-looking assumptions
- [ ] Management projections have been haircut where appropriate

## 3. Framework Appropriateness

- [ ] The right valuation framework was chosen for the situation (CRITICAL)
- [ ] DCF is not applied to companies where cash flows are too speculative without scenario weighting
- [ ] Comparable companies are genuinely comparable (sector, size, growth profile)
- [ ] Leverage ratios are appropriate for the company's cash flow stability
- [ ] M&A synergies are categorized as revenue vs. cost and discounted appropriately

## 4. Downside & Stress Testing

- [ ] A downside/bear scenario is presented (not just base case) (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Sensitivity analysis shows key value drivers and their impact
- [ ] Leverage stress test shows debt serviceability in downside
- [ ] Break-even analysis shows what needs to be true for the deal/investment to work
- [ ] Key risks are explicitly identified and sized

## 5. Actionability

- [ ] Clear recommendation with specific action (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Decision criteria are explicit (what would change this recommendation)
- [ ] Next steps are prioritized and specific
- [ ] Timeline and ownership are suggested where relevant
- [ ] "So what" is answered — not just what is true, but what to do

## 6. Clarity & Communication

- [ ] Executive summary is present and self-contained (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Non-finance stakeholders can follow the core logic
- [ ] Charts and tables have clear titles, axis labels, and units
- [ ] Jargon is explained or avoided
- [ ] The document answers the question it was asked

## 7. Data Room / Source Quality

- [ ] All data sources are cited
- [ ] Market data uses reputable sources (Bloomberg, CapIQ, CB Insights, sector reports)
- [ ] Financial data is LTM or NTM with clear dating
- [ ] Adjustments to reported financials are explained
